Instructions

In this experiment we will ask you to make judgments about videos of physical interactions. The interactions will take place in the rectangular space shown below.

In each video you will see some of the following four objects:

  • a green ball (labeled with A)
  • a grey ball (labeled with B)
  • an unlabeled blue ball
  • a box
Not all of the objects will appear in every video, however every video will contain ball A and ball B. Objects will either start on screen or enter from the right. The balls can exit the space through the red exit on the left but only when it is not blocked by the brown gate. The position of the brown gate can be changed by pressing either of the green buttons next to it.

Take a look at the example video below by clicking the "Play Video" button. You can watch the video again by clicking on "Replay".

Instructions

In the first part of the experiment you will see a series of videos like the one we just showed you. For each video we will ask you to choose a sentence that best describes what happened in the video. You will be able to choose one of these four descriptions:

  • Ball A caused Ball B to go through the red exit.
  • Ball A enabled Ball B to go through the red exit.
  • Ball A affected Ball B's going through the red exit.
  • Ball A made no difference to Ball B's going through the red exit.
You will watch each video twice before making a selection. You may replay the video as many times as you like before deciding.

Below we have included a screenshot of an example trial. The participant has selected the "caused" description as the most appropriate for this clip.

This first part of the experiment will consist of 4 short trials. Before you start this part we will ask you a few quick questions to make sure the setup is clear. If you answer one or more of these questions incorrectly, you will be redirected to take another look at the instructions.
